🏰 Forts & Territory

Expand your empire by capturing forts and defending your territory.

Types of Forts

Three types of forts exist on the map:

🟠 Unowned Monster Forts

Owner: None (wild)
Garrison: 2–16 monsters (random star level 1–6)
Capture reward: Ownership + loot (Gold, Metal, Food, Timber)
Difficulty: Good beginner targets. Similar to monster camps but permanent.

🟢 Your Forts

Owner: You
Garrison: Your troops + defense buildings
Defensive state: When attacked, your troops + defenses fight back
Building limit: 4–10 slots (random at capture)

🔴 Enemy Forts

Owner: Another player
Garrison: Their troops + defense buildings
Capture reward: Ownership + loot
Difficulty: Highly variable. Depends on player strength.

Capturing Forts

To capture a fort, you must defeat its garrison (all defending troops and buildings). Here's what happens:

1️⃣

Attack the fort

Dispatch your army to the target fort on the world map.

2️⃣

Battle resolves

Your army fights the garrison. If you win, all defending buildings are destroyed.

3️⃣

Ownership transfers

The fort turns your colour. You now own it. A new Command Centre spawns automatically.

4️⃣

Loot collected

Resources from the defeated garrison are added to your reserve.

⚠️ Important: When a fort is captured, ALL buildings are destroyed. You start with only Command Centre. The new owner (you) can rebuild from scratch.

Defending Your Forts

Once you own a fort, you want to keep it. Defending is crucial:

Station Troops

Keep an army idle at the fort. When someone attacks, your troops spawn on the battlefield and fight the invader's army.

Build Defense Buildings

Cannons and Archer Towers spawn during battle, dealing heavy damage. They consume ammo per turn. Build at least 1–2 defensively crucial forts.

Don't Abandon Resources

If a fort has tons of accumulated resources, it becomes a target. Regularly collect resources to move them to your castle.

Losing a Fort

If an attacker defeats your garrison, they capture the fort. What happens:

Your troops: Destroyed (part of the battle loss).

Buildings: All destroyed (as with any capture).

Fort itself: Transfers to the new owner's control.

Your loot: Captured. The new owner gains resources.

Recovery: You lost a fort, but you still have your castle and any other forts. Focus on rebuilding troops and trying to recapture.

Multi-Fort Strategy

As you grow, you'll own multiple forts. Here's a typical layout strategy:

Castle: Primary economy (4–5 production buildings, 1 training building for overflow).

Fort 1 (nearby): Secondary army (2 Garrison/Stable for troop production).

Fort 2 (far): Heavily defended (Cannons, Archer Towers, garrison troops).

Fort 3+ (strategic): Specialized (relay outposts for jumping attacks, resource overflow, etc.).

Fort Management Tips

  • ✓ Capture nearby forts first. Easier to defend and resupply.
  • ✓ Prioritize rebuilding production. A captured fort with farms = free resources.
  • ✓ Garrison key forts. Your most valuable forts should have idle troops + defenses.
  • ✓ Collect resources regularly. Move loot to castle to prevent raids.
  • ✓ Recapture lost territory. Use your forts as staging points for revenge attacks.
  • ✓ Join a clan to coordinate defense. Clan-mates can help defend or recapture.
