⚔ Armies & Training

How to train, manage, and deploy your forces effectively.

How Training Works

Military buildings produce soldiers using a queue system. Each building trains one unit at a time (sequentially), but you can queue multiple units.

1️⃣

Select a building

Click on a Garrison (Barracks) or Stable at your castle or fort.

2️⃣

Queue units

Click "Queue Longbowman" or "Queue Hussar". Resources are deducted immediately.

3️⃣

Wait for completion

A timer shows how long until the unit is ready (60s for Longbowman, 90s for Hussar at level 1).

4️⃣

Automatic deployment

Completed units are automatically idle at that location, ready to be dispatched.

Unit Types

Two human unit types are trainable; monsters spawn naturally:

🏹 Longbowman

Source: Garrison (Barracks)

Training time: 60s (level 1)

Cost: 25 Food, 10 Gold

Type: Ranged. Decent damage, medium speed.

Pros: Cheap, beginner-friendly.

Cons: Lower damage per unit than Hussar.

🐎 Hussar

Source: Stable

Training time: 90s (level 1)

Cost: 30 Food, 15 Gold

Type: Cavalry. Fast, mobile.

Pros: Double speed, good for hit-and-run.

Cons: Slower training, higher cost.

Monsters: Trolls and Wraiths spawn naturally in forts and camps. Check the Troops Index for full stats.

Army Composition

You can dispatch mixed armies (Longbowmen + Hussars together). Different compositions suit different scenarios:

🎯 Balanced Mix (Recommended for beginners)

Example: 8 Longbowmen + 4 Hussars

Pros: Versatile. Handles most situations.

Cons: Not optimized for anything specific.

⚡ Ranged Heavy

Example: 15 Longbowmen

Pros: Sustained damage from range. Good for sieges.

Cons: Vulnerable to fast units.

🐴 Fast Strike

Example: 10 Hussars

Pros: Mobile, hit fast.

Cons: Lower durability; risky.

Troop Management

📊 Viewing Your Troops

Go to your castle or fort and click "Troop Details" in the Command Centre. You'll see a count of all units at that location, grouped by type.

📤 Dispatching Armies

On the world map, click a target and select the preset or manually choose units. Confirm the dispatch. Troops are immediately deducted from your location.

🗑️ Dismissing Troops

In the troop details panel, you can dismiss troops to free up training slots. You'll get a 50% refund of their training cost.

💡 Dismissal tip: If you trained the wrong unit type or need resources urgently, dismiss troops. You lose 50% of the cost, but it's better than sitting on idle units.

Formations (Presets)

Instead of manually selecting units each time, you can save presets (named formations). When attacking, just pick a preset and dispatch instantly.

To create a preset: Go to the battle simulator, set up your army, and save the formation. You can reuse it from the world map when attacking.

Pro Training Tips

  • ✓ Queue in bulk. Queue 10 Longbowmen at once; they'll train sequentially.
  • ✓ Upgrade military buildings. Level-2 Garrison trains units in 30s (vs 60s). Game-changer.
  • ✓ Train while building. While your farms are generating resources, queue troops.
  • ✓ Multiple locations = parallel training. Train Longbowmen at castle, Hussars at a fort simultaneously.
  • ✓ Don't over-train. Training costs resources. Only train what you can afford to dispatch.
