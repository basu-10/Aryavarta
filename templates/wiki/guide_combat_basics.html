⚡ Combat Basics

Learn how battles work and basic strategy for your first wins.

The Attack Flow

1️⃣

Select target on the map

Click any entity (monster camp, fort, etc.)

2️⃣

Choose your army

Pick a preset or select units manually from the popup.

3️⃣

Confirm dispatch

Review the battle preview (your army vs their garrison). Click Attack if you're confident.

4️⃣

Travel countdown

Your army travels. You see a countdown timer (e.g., "Arriving in 5s").

5️⃣

Auto-resolve

When the timer hits zero, the battle runs and results are shown instantly.

Battle Preview

Before you attack, the game shows a preview of your army vs. the defender's garrison. This gives you a chance to estimate outcomes.

Your army: e.g., "8 Longbowmen, 4 Hussars"

Defender garrison: e.g., "3 Trolls"

Battle odds: A rough estimate (usually shown as "Strong advantage" or "Evenly matched")

Green = you're favored. Red = risky. Yellow = 50/50. Use this to decide whether to attack.

⚠️ Important: Consequences

Your army is deducted IMMEDIATELY when you dispatch, not after the battle resolves.

If you lose the attack, those troops are gone forever. You don't get them back. The defender keeps the fort, but any defending troops killed in the battle are also permanently lost from the garrison.

Strategy: Only attack targets you're confident you can beat. If you're unsure, skip the attack.

Battle Outcomes

After the battle resolves, one of these happens:

The battle results page is authoritative: it shows the exact survivors from the fight. If you win, only the surviving attackers are returned to your origin. If the defender wins, the attacker is destroyed and defender losses are removed from the garrison.

✓ You Win vs. Monster Camp

Reward: Gold + Metal loot (usually 50–500 depending on difficulty stars).
Result: Camp is deactivated. A new camp respawns elsewhere later.

✓ You Win vs. Monster Fort

Reward: You capture the fort (turns green). Loot is added to your resources.
Result: You now own the fort and can build there.

✗ You Lose

Penalty: Your entire army is destroyed. Nothing else happens.
Defense: If you were attacking a fort, the defender doesn't take damage to their fort itself, but any defending troops killed in the battle are removed from the garrison.
Recovery: Go back to training troops.

Tips for Your First Attack

  • ✓ Start with 1-star targets. They have only 2–3 defenders. Your first 5 Longbowmen should win.
  • ✓ Use the battle preview. If odds look bad, don't attack. Come back with more troops.
  • ✓ Attack monster camps first. They're easier than forts and give decent loot.
  • ✓ Scout nearby first. Short travel time = less risky (fewer things can go wrong).
  • ✓ Build momentum. First win = confidence + resources for more armies.
