🚀 Your First Hour in BattleCells

A step-by-step guide to get you from zero to your first successful attack.

Step 1: Understand Your Starting Position

When you log in, you're at your castle (blue dot on the map). You start with:

  • 🌾 200 Food
  • 🪵 200 Timber
  • 💛 100 Gold
  • ⚙️ 100 Metal

Your castle can hold up to 9 buildings in slots (arranged in a 3×3 grid). One slot is occupied by the Command Centre, which is automatic and required.

Step 2: Build Your First Farm

To grow your resources, you need production buildings. Let's start with a Farm.

Farm (Resource Building)

Cost: 50 Timber, 50 Gold

Build time: 30 seconds

Production: 0.05 Food/second (level 1)

Max level: 10

How to build: Navigate to your castle (tab shows "Castle"). Click on an empty slot and select "Farm". Confirm the build. The farm will take 30 seconds to complete.

💡 Tip: Build time is wasted waiting. For your first farm, it's fine to wait. But keep in mind that you can queue multiple buildings or build multiple buildings at once (if you have enough resources).

Step 3: Build More Production

Once your farm is built, build one of each:

Lumber Mill

Cost: 50 Food, 50 Gold

Produces: Timber (0.05/s)

Merchant

Cost: 50 Food, 50 Timber

Produces: Gold (0.05/s)

Mine

Cost: 50 Food, 50 Timber

Produces: Metal (0.05/s)

Farm 2

Cost: 50 Timber, 50 Gold

Produces: Food (0.05/s)

This gives you a balanced economy. Now collect your first batch of resources. Go to your castle and click the Collect button in the resources panel to harvest accumulated production.

Step 4: Train Your First Troops

Now you have resources flowing in. Let's train some soldiers. You need military buildings:

Garrison (Barracks)

Cost: 100 Timber, 100 Gold, 50 Metal

Produces: Longbowman (60s each)

Training Cost: 25 Food, 10 Gold

Stable

Cost: 100 Food, 100 Timber, 50 Metal

Produces: Hussar (90s each)

Training Cost: 30 Food, 15 Gold

How to train: Build a Garrison or Stable at your castle. Once built, click on it to see the training queue. Select "Queue Longbowman" or "Queue Hussar". The unit will start training immediately and complete in 60–90 seconds.

💡 Tip: Start with 5–10 Longbowmen (ranged). They're cheaper and good for learning.

Step 5: Find Your First Target

With troops ready, let's find something to attack. Navigate to the World Map (from the main menu). You'll see:

● Blue: Your castle
● Green: Your captured forts
● Red: Enemy player forts
● Orange: Unowned monster forts (great for beginners!)
● Purple: Monster camps

Look for a 1-star (★) orange monster fort or purple monster camp close to your castle. Distance affects travel time, so pick something nearby.

Step 6: Your First Attack

Click on a monster camp or fort to see a popup with its stats:

Difficulty: ★ (1 star)

Defenders: 2–3 Trolls (typical for 1-star)

Travel distance: Example: 5 cells away = ~5 seconds travel

Rewards: Gold and Metal (for camps), or claimed fort (for forts)

Click the Attack button. Select your prepared troops (e.g., 5 Longbowmen). The app will show you a battle preview — if you're confident, launch the attack.

✓ Success! After the travel time expires (you'll see a countdown), your browser will auto-resolve the battle and show you the results.

Monster Difficulty Reference

Monster entities spawn with random difficulty levels (stars). Higher stars = more defenders + harder units:

Stars Defender Count Recommendation
★ 2–3 Easy. ~5 troops to win.
★★ 4–5 Moderate. ~10–15 troops.
★★★ 6–8 Challenging. ~20 troops or upgrades.
★★★★+ 9+ Hard. Come back when stronger.

What's Next?

  • ✓ Repeat: Build more troops, attack more camps/forts, grow your economy
  • ✓ Upgrade your production buildings to level 2+ to accelerate growth
  • ✓ Capture a fort to expand your territory and build a second army there
  • ✓ Form or join a clan to coordinate with other players
