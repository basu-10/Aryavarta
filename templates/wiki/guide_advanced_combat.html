🎯 Advanced Combat

Master formations, loot calculations, and tactical strategy.

How Battles Work (Phases)

Battles are deterministic and run through 6 phases over multiple ticks (up to 200 ticks max). Understanding these helps you predict outcomes:

Phase 1–6: Combat Cycle

Each phase, units move across a battlefield grid and attack. Ranged units (Longbowmen, Wraiths) can attack from range. Melee units (Hussars, Trolls) must advance and engage up close.

Battlefield Grid

The battle happens on a 9-wide grid (columns 0–8). Your army (Team A) spawns in columns 0–3. Enemy army (Team B) spawns in columns 5–8. Column 4 is neutral/no-man's-land.

Speed Advantage

Units move based on their speed stat. Faster units close gaps and attack sooner. Hussars (fast) will engage before Longbowmen (slower).

Ending Condition

Battle ends when one side is fully eliminated, or 200 ticks pass (stalemate = attacker loses by default).

Formation Strategy

How you arrange your army affects the outcome. Different formations suit different opponents:

🎯 Balanced (Recommended)

Composition: Mix ranged and cavalry (e.g., 60% Longbowmen, 40% Hussars)
Advantage: Versatile vs. all enemy types.
When to use: Scouting, unsure what you're facing.

⚡ Heavy Ranged

Composition: Mostly Longbowmen (80%+)
Advantage: Sustained damage. Kites and overwhelms slow enemies.
Weakness: Vulnerable to fast units that close gaps.
When to use: Vs. Trolls or static defenses.

🐴 Fast Cavalry

Composition: Mostly Hussars (70%+)
Advantage: Engages first. Overwhelms before enemy reaches halfway.
Weakness: Lower HP and durability.
When to use: Vs. slow ranged armies. Quick hit-and-run attacks.

The Battle Simulator

Before attacking, you can use the battle simulator to practice and test formations:

Access: From the main menu, go to "Battle Simulator".

Simulate: Create a custom scenario (your army vs. a known enemy garrison).

Run: Simulate the battle 1+ times to see outcomes.

Save: Save successful formations as presets for reuse in real attacks.

Pro tip: Always simulate before high-risk attacks. The preview gives you valuable data.

Loot & Resource Gains

When you win an attack, you gain resources based on what you defeated:

Monster Camp Victory

Loot: Gold + Metal (50–500+ depending on star level)
Formula: Scales with difficulty

Fort Capture

Loot: All accumulated resources at the fort (Gold, Metal, Food, Timber)
Plus: Building loot from any structures you destroyed

Cost vs. Reward

A victory is profitable if total loot > army training cost. Example: Winning a 3-star camp nets ~200 loot; training your 10-troop army cost ~300. Break-even on resources, but you expanded territory (forts).

Counter Strategies

Different unit types and formations counter each other:

→ Trolls (tanky): Counter with Longbowmen. Sustained ranged damage bypasses armor.
→ Wraiths (fast ranged): Counter with Hussars. Close the gap before taking sustained damage.
→ Mixed garrison: Use balanced composition or test in simulator first.
→ Defense buildings (Cannons, Towers): They're stationary but powerful. Use speed to overwhelm before they kill your army.

Risk Management

  • ✓ Always simulate first. A 30-second test saves you a lost army.
  • ✓ Look for 70%+ win odds. Don't gamble on 50/50 battles unless desperate.
  • ✓ Diversify targets. Mix monster camps (safe) with forts (riskier but more rewarding).
  • ✓ Keep reserves. Don't spend all your troops on one attack. Maintain defense.
  • ✓ Retreat-build strategy. If you lose, rebuild troops and try a different approach.
  • ✓ Learn from losses. Review battle replays to understand what went wrong.
