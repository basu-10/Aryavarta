🏗 Buildings Overview

Learn what each building type does and when to build them.

Building Categories

🌾 Resource Buildings

Generate passive resources over time. Essential for your economy.

Farm

Produces Food. Build 1–2 at your castle. Cost: 50 Timber, 50 Gold

Lumber Mill

Produces Timber. Essential. Cost: 50 Food, 50 Gold

Merchant

Produces Gold. Build 1–2. Cost: 50 Food, 50 Timber

Mine

Produces Metal. Build 1–2. Cost: 50 Food, 50 Timber

⚔ Military Buildings

Train soldiers for your army. Required to progress.

Garrison (Barracks)

Trains: Longbowman (60s, 25F 10G)

Cost: 100 Timber, 100 Gold, 50 Metal

Good for: Ranged support, beginner army

Stable

Trains: Hussar (90s, 30F 15G)

Cost: 100 Food, 100 Timber, 50 Metal

Good for: Fast units, hit-and-run tactics

🛡 Defense Buildings

Protect your locations during attacks. Stationary but powerful.

Cannon

Role: Heavy single-target damage

Ammo: Cannon Ball (must be loaded)

Note: Stationary. Spawned during battle.

Archer Tower

Role: Sustained ranged damage

Ammo: Arrow (must be loaded)

Note: Stationary. Spawned during battle.

How defense works: Defense buildings are spawned at your location during enemy attacks. They consume ammo each turn they fire. Defend forts and castles you want to keep.

✨ Special Buildings

Command Centre

Automatic. Spawned at every location (castle + forts). Slot 0 is always occupied by this. Build time: instant. Indestructible.

Building Slot Strategy

Each location (castle or fort) starts with a maximum number of building slots (4–10 for forts, 9 for castles). Slot 0 is always Command Centre. Here's a typical early-game layout:

Slot 0: Command Centre (automatic)

Slot 1–3: Farm, Lumber Mill, Merchant (production)

Slot 4: Mine (production)

Slot 5–6: Garrison, Stable (military)

Slot 7–8: Cannon, Archer Tower (defense)

Pro tip: As you capture more forts, use each location's slot count for specialization. Castles = economy. Secondary forts = military barracks. Forward forts = defense focus.

Upgrading Buildings

Every building can be upgraded from level 1 to level 10. Upgrades are CRITICAL:

For resource buildings: Doubling resource production is exponential. Level 2 = 2× output, Level 3 = 4× output, Level 10 = 512× output!

For military buildings: Upgrade reduces training time. A level-2 Garrison trains Longbowmen in ~30 seconds instead of 60.

Cost increases: Higher-level upgrades cost more resources, but the payoff is huge.

Strategy: Early game = prioritize production building upgrades. Mid-game = balance with military. Late game = upgrade everything.
