🗺 World Map Navigation

Explore a 50×50 grid, find targets, and plan your attacks.

The 50×50 Grid

BattleCells plays on a procedurally placed 50×50 world map. Each cell can hold one entity: your castle, your fort, someone else's fort, a monster camp, or a wild monster fort.

Coordinates & Distance

Each cell has an (x, y) coordinate. The distance formula used is Chebyshev distance (also called "chessboard distance"), which favors moving diagonally. Maximum distance on the map: ~50 cells.

Entity Types & Colours

Entities are colour-coded. Quickly spot what you're looking at:

🔵

Blue: Your Castle

Your home base. Indestructible, cannot be attacked.

🟢

Green: Your Forts

Territory you've captured. Defendable. Can be lost if defeated.

🔴

Red: Enemy Forts

Forts owned by other players. Attackable. Capture them for loot + territory.

🟠

Orange: Wild Monster Forts

Unowned forts with monster garrisons. Easy targets for beginners.

🟣

Purple: Monster Camps

Non-territorial targets. Attackable for loot. Despawn after defeat.

⬜

Gray: Empty

Unoccupied cells. Nothing to attack here.

Viewing Entity Details

Click on any entity on the map to open a popup with detailed information:

Fort/Camp name

Owner (if any)

Difficulty (★ to ★★★★★)

Garrison (unit count and types)

Distance from your location

Estimated travel time

Attack button to dispatch your army

Travel Time Calculation

Armies take time to travel. The formula depends on distance and the slowest unit's speed:

travel_seconds = distance × (WORLD_TRAVEL_SECONDS_PER_CELL / slowest_unit_speed)

Example: Traveling 5 cells with Longbowmen might take ~5–10 seconds depending on unit speeds. Closer targets arrive faster. Always pick nearby targets if you want quick results.

💡 Pro tip: You can see the estimated travel time in the attack popup before you commit. This lets you plan when to dispatch attacks.

Map Controls

Click a cell: View entity details or empty land.

Drag/pan: Move around the map to explore.

Zoom +/–: Use buttons in the map header to zoom in/out. Larger cells are easier to click.

Ctrl + Mouse wheel: Zoom with keyboard shortcut.

Reset button: Return to 100% zoom.

Strategic Scanning

When looking for targets:

  • 1. Check nearby cells first. Short travel time = faster rewards.
  • 2. Target 1-star camps/forts as a beginner. They're much easier to defeat.
  • 3. Avoid red (enemy) forts until you're ready. Players defend them with troops + defenses.
  • 4. Scout larger stars later. As you grow, tackle higher-difficulty entities.
  • 5. Look for clusters of orange forts. Multiple 1–2 star forts nearby = efficient grinding.
